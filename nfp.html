The NON-Farm Payrolls (NFP)

The Non-Farm Payroll (NFP) is a major U.S. economic report released on the first Friday of every month. It measures how many jobs were added or lost (excluding farm work and a few sectors). Because it reflects the strength of the U.S. economy, it strongly affects the U.S. dollar, gold, and major forex pairs. When the NFP numbers come out, markets usually move fast. Traders watch the report to compare the actual results with expectations, along with the unemployment rate and average hourly earnings. Big differences between expected and actual numbers often cause sharp price movements. NFP is important because it guides economic outlook, influences interest rates, and helps traders understand possible market trends. Many traders wait for the initial spike to settle before reacting, since NFP volatility can be high.
